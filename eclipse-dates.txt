The next total solar eclipses occurring worldwide over the next decade are:

*   August 12, 2026: Visible in Greenland, Iceland, and Spain.
*   August 2, 2027: Visible in North Africa (Egypt, Libya, Algeria, Morocco), Spain, and the Middle East (Saudi Arabia, Yemen).
*   July 22, 2028: Visible in Australia and New Zealand.
*   November 25, 2030: Visible in Botswana, South Africa, and Australia.
*   November 14, 2031: A hybrid eclipse (total in some areas) visible in the Pacific Ocean.
*   March 30, 2033: Visible in Alaska and Russia.
*   March 20, 2034: Visible in Central Africa and South Asia.
*   September 2, 2035: Visible in China, North Korea, and Japan.

For those in the contiguous United States, the next total solar eclipse will not occur until August 22, 2044 (visible in Montana and North Dakota), followed by a major coast-to-coast eclipse on August 12, 2045.
